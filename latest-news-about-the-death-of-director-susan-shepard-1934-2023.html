As per the family’s announcement, Suzanne Shepherd passed away at her private residence in New York on November 17, 2023, at the age of 89. The cause of death remains undisclosed.

Her family expressed, “We are deeply saddened to announce that Suzanne Shepherd peacefully passed away at her home, surrounded by family and loved ones this morning. She lived a meaningful life as a talented artist and a wonderful mother, grandmother, and great-grandmother.”

Brief Overview of Suzanne Shepherd’s Life

Suzanne Shepherd was born in 1934 in New York, USA. She studied acting under the guidance of Professor Sanford Meisner and later became the first woman to teach Meisner’s acting program.

Throughout her acting career, Suzanne Shepherd was recognized for roles such as:

Mrs. Scarlini in the film “Requiem for a Dream” (2000)
Aunt Tweedy in the movie “Mystic Pizza” (1988)
Karen’s mother in the film “Goodfellas” (1990)
Carmela Soprano’s mother, Mary DeAngelis, in the hit HBO television series “The Sopranos”
Assistant Principal in the movie “Uncle Buck” (1989)
Big Ethel in the film “A Dirty Shame” (2004)

Apart from her acting career, Suzanne Shepherd co-founded the Compass Players theater group in the early 1960s alongside actors Alan Alda and Alan Arkin.

Her daughter, Kate Shepherd, also pursued acting and became an artist in her own right.
