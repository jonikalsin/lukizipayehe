What is the Professional Career of Amber Riley?

In the comedy-drama television series “Glee,” Amber Riley was cast as Mercedes Jones in 2008. She participated in all but one of the show’s six seasons as a key cast member; in season 5, she received a guest star credit. Riley performed a number of solo songs over the entire series, such as “Respect,” “Bust Your Windows,” and “Hate on Me.”

Riley also played Dr. Frank-N-Furter and sang the lead vocal on the song “Sweet Transvestite” in the Rocky Horror Glee Show episode, receiving generally positive reviews from critics. Riley received a Screen Actors Guild Award for her portrayal of Mercedes Jones, for which she was nominated for three NAACP Image Awards and three Teen Choice Awards. On April 3, 2014, Riley’s “Colourblind” made its live debut on The Queen Latifah Show. The song was written by Emeli Sandé, Claude Kelly, and Steve Mac and released as a digital download on April 8, 2014. It was expected to be the lead single from her debut album.

In 2017, Riley joined British singers Cassidy Janson and Beverley Knight to form the Leading Ladies, a supergroup for musical theater. Their debut album, Songs from the Stage, was released on November 17, 2017, and it features covers of songs from shows including Cats, Beautiful, and Rent, among others. On February 28, 2020, Riley announced that she has completed recording the songs for her debut extended play. The EP and its six components become accessible on October 2, 2020. On October 8, 2020, she made a music video for the song “Creepin.”

Awards and Achievements

Amber Riley has received twelve nominations in total, four of which have resulted in prizes. She has won four awards, including the 2010 Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series for Glee, the Whatsonstage.com Award for Best Actress in a Musical, the 2017 Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical, and the 2017 Evening Standard Award for Best Musical Performance for Dreamgirls.
